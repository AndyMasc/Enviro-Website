The Issue

Get to grips with the climate issues plaguing New Zealand.

The Gist of it

Climate change poses significant risks to New Zealand, including more extreme weather like droughts and floods, changing rainfall patterns, and threats to native ecosystems and biodiversity. The issue is being addressed through government policies such as the Climate Change Response (Zero Carbon) Amendment Act, but impacts on industries like primary production and the environment are already being felt and are projected to worsen without action.

The Impacts of it

Climate change is affecting New Zealand through increased temperatures, sea-level rise, and changes in rainfall patterns, leading to more extreme weather events like droughts and floods. These changes threaten agriculture, infrastructure, and native ecosystems, while also impacting human health and well-being. Key effects include less stable water supplies, increased erosion and landslides, greater pest and disease risks, and stress on marine and terrestrial biodiversity.
